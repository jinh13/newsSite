STAR STUDENTS

Highlighting stellar WUSTL students who have made an impact on the student body, campus, and the St. Louis community. Click on the students' names to read the news articles they were featured in!

SCHOOL OF ARTS AND SCIENCE

Joshua Aiken was one of 32 Americans chosen as a Rhodes Scholar at the University of Oxford in England. He plans to earn master's degrees in US history and refugee studies.

Nishanth Uli won in the preliminary round of Jeopardy and won $22,801. He studied biology and history at WUSTL and now attends Ohio State University School of Medicine.

SCHOOL OF ENGINEERING

Darius Calliet, Akeda Hosten, and Deanna Shipley won the MasterCard Hackathon in 2014. They also won $10,000 as the winning prize.

Kevin Hays, a member of the WUSTL swim team, set the record by solving the rubik's cube in two minutes and five seconds in a dunk tank.

SCHOOL OF BUSINESS

Andrew Glantz and Jacob Mohrmann started FoodShare, a startup that donates a meal to a person in need through Operation Food Search for every meal a user orders through the app.

Blake Marggraff, Avik Som, Evan Huang, and Joe McDonald started Epharmix, a health technology startup that creates condition-specific digital interventions for medical care. They raised $765,000 from a group of five investors.

SCHOOL OF ART AND ARCHITECTURE

Sarah Mae Ettinger is a fashion designer who has interned at Marchesa, Michael Kores, and Kate Spade. She was the recipient of the 2014 Fashion Group International (FGI) St. Louis Scholarship Award.

Jacob Lenard, Felicia Podberesky, and Chantal Strasburger founded Armour Magazine, a fashion magazine focused on highlighting the eclectic styles of WUSTL students.
